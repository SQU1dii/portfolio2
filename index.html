My Childhood

Childhood Roots: The Logic Behind the Code

My childhood was a foundational stage for my passion for Computer Science. It began not with code, but with an insatiable curiosity about how things work.
I was drawn to the logical systems in LEGOs and the strategic demands of puzzles and strategy games. These hobbies trained me in algorithmic thinking and problem decomposition—the art of breaking complex challenges into manageable, rule-based steps.
My early fascination with HALO computer-game evolved from mere consumption to wanting to understand the underlying mechanics. The "Aha!" moment came when I realized this love for logic and structure could be applied to a computer, transforming abstract thought into tangible, functional applications. My childhood was less about specific technical skills and more about developing the curious, systematic mindset that defines my approach to CS today.
